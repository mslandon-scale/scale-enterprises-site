How It Started

Scraped Knees, Scar Tissue, and the Notes I Wish I'd Had

5 min read
Matthew Landon
Founder, Scale Enterprises

My name is Matthew Landon, and I've been building businesses for a long time. Ever since I was a kid. One of my first was as a 3rd grader, buying toolbox kits and selling the finished product. Ever since I've done landscaping, agencies, real estate, e-commerce, health and wellness, and consulting.

Every time I would step out onto the field of entrepreneurship the goal is to not create a job for myself with extra steps. Not a lifestyle business. A real enterprise, the kind that changes something in the world and gives me the wealth, freedom, and control. A bigger game.

It has cost me. A lot.

The Swings That Missed

I've taken swings in real estate and blown it. I've built agencies and blown it. I've launched coaching and watched it fizzle out. I've had ventures crash and burn in ways that still sting.

I've scraped both knees raw and then gotten up and scraped them again — because every time something collapsed, I was certain the next one would be different.

Most of the time it wasn't. And the worst part wasn't the money or the time. It was the quiet realization afterward, every single time, that I hadn't actually known what I was missing, why it didn't work, or even why some did work.

At one point I tripled an organization's lead flow in seven days from 40 to 120 qualified leads a week. It worked beautifully. But even as I was doing it, I only grasped some of why the pieces connected. I couldn't repeat it on demand, and I couldn't explain it cleanly to anyone else. A win I couldn't replicate was still, in the long run, a loss waiting to happen.

So I stopped guessing. I studied, obsessively. I knew that if I was going to succeed I couldn't continue throwing spaghetti at a wall and hoping it stuck. I read hundreds of books. I spent real money to get in the right rooms. I learned from the people who had actually done it, not the people who talked about it.

A Pattern That Refused to Break

I reverse-engineered every company I admired down to the core. A pattern emerged that I could not un-see.

The Pattern

The ventures that won had these exact elements locked in. The ones that failed had almost none. Not some. Almost none. Once I saw the pattern I couldn't sleep for days because I knew that I had discovered something that would change me and my family's life.

This playbook you're reading is not a prescription. It's not me telling you what you should do. It's simply my notes. It's the tool I built for myself, out of every win and every loss, and it's how I'm building companies now. If I looked at a business and saw these elements installed, I'd put serious time and money into it, because I know firsthand the odds of that business succeeding go up dramatically.

I have compiled 56 things that I believe are paramount that any business (especially service businesses) I own must have for its success.

Before I hand you the tactics and the checklist, you must see the bigger picture first. The real value of these 56 elements only becomes clear when you understand the map they live inside — the pattern I kept seeing in every company that made it.

This playbook has given me the power to take control of my businesses so that I can reach my full potential. I am confident that it will do the same for you.
