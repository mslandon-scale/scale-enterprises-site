The 5 Stages Every Enterprise Moves Through

8 min read

After years of studying operators who actually made it, one pattern refused to break — every enterprise that crossed nine figures moved through the same five stages. Not similar. The same.

Tony Robbins, Alex Hormozi, Russell Brunson, Grant Cardone, Brandon Dawson, Ryan Pineda, Pace Morby — they each discovered it, survived it, scaled through it. But none of them have ever put it together as one full, repeatable framework.

Critical Rule

Each stage has a revenue range, a core theme, and a specific job to be done. Skip a stage, or try to play one stage's game with another stage's tools, and the whole thing stalls.

Stage 1 $0 → 6 Figures

Value Creation

The only job here is proving the market wants what you're selling. Not systems, not team, not brand — just value. Can you make something real that people will pay for, repeatedly? Everything else is a distraction until this is locked.

Stage 2 6 → 7 Figures

Systems

The thing that works has to stop living inside the founder's head. Documented processes, CRM flows, fulfillment SOPs, financial rigor. It's where the business stops depending on the founder to function.

Stage 3 7 → 8 Figures

Enterprise Leadership

This is the stage the whole playbook was built for. Every element on the checklist is installed, the Rule of Three is in seat, and the cash is flowing. This is where a business becomes a real enterprise and a founder becomes an owner.

When the checklist is fully installed and the three litmus tests are green, the business becomes sellable. A stand-alone sale at 5–8× EBITDA becomes realistic.

Stage 4 8 → 9 Figures

Platform

You keep everything from the checklist, add middle management beneath the leadership team, and bring every major function in-house — marketing, finance, tech, operations, people, sales strategy, and legal. The business becomes vertically integrated and completely self-contained.

Stage 5 9 → 10 Figures

Private Equity

By the time you're here, the operating business runs itself. Your job shifts entirely to strategy — roll-ups, M&A, franchising, vertical integration, and acquiring competitors. It becomes a game of deal flow, capitalization, and integration.

"All roads lead to private equity."
— Matthew Landon

The Map Isn't Enough

I had a framework proven across every industry I studied — coaching, real estate, e-commerce, services, SaaS, manufacturing. But knowing you need to go from Stage 1 to Stage 2 doesn't tell you how to actually do it.

What were the specific things these operators were doing inside each stage? What tactics? What systems? What hires?

So I went back to the study. I tore apart the companies. And I kept a running list of every element I saw repeated across the winners. That list became 56 elements — and it's what you're about to see next.
